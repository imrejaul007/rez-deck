The Problem

Why do SMBs fail in India?

80% fail within 5 years. The #1 reason: they never learn who their customers are. They can't answer "who visited my store last week?" They can't predict which offers work. They can't do targeted marketing. They guess. They burn money on ads that don't work. They compete on price alone and get killed by aggregators taking 20-30%.

Why don't aggregators solve this?

Aggregators (Swiggy, Zomato, Dunzo) own the transaction data. Merchants are dependent. They pay 20-30% and get NO customer data. They can't reach customers directly. If they leave the platform, they lose everything. Aggregators profit; merchants struggle.

Why haven't loyalty programs worked?

Single-merchant loyalty is broken. A coffee shop loyalty card means nothing if you also visit 5 other cafes. Customers want rewards that work everywhere. Until now, only credit cards and aggregators had the scale for multi-merchant rewards. REZ brings this to every local merchant.

Why is now the right time?

Four tailwinds align:
1) UPI is ready: 22.6B transactions/month. Payments are frictionless.
2) QR is mainstream: Customers scan QR codes without thinking.
3) ML is cheap: AI infrastructure costs 90% less than 5 years ago.
4) Merchants are desperate: Post-COVID, they want direct customer relationships, not dependency on platforms.

Product & Moat

What exactly are you building?

REZ is the behavioral graph of local commerce. We give every merchant their own customer data layer, powered by AI.

How: Merchant pays 5% → Customer gets 5% cashback → Customer shares on Instagram/YouTube → Gets bonus coins.

The result: Merchants finally own customer relationships. They know who buys what, when, how often. AI predicts behavior. Growth becomes predictable.

What's your moat? Why can't Google copy this?

Three moats:

1) Data flywheel: More transactions = better AI = better predictions = more merchants. This compounds over time.

2) Behavioral graph: We learn cross-merchant patterns. Google knows you searched for coffee. We know you visited 3 cafes, 2 salons, and 1 gym last week. That's irreplaceable.

3) Local density: Network effects are hyperlocal. A cluster of 200 merchants in one neighborhood is worth more than 200 scattered merchants. This takes time to build.

Is this just another loyalty app?

No. Traditional loyalty apps are single-merchant stamp cards. REZ is infrastructure.

Think: We're not building a credit card; we're building the Visa network. Every merchant accepts us. Every customer uses us everywhere. That's the difference.

Not: QR startup, wallet app, loyalty program
We are: Merchant-owned commerce intelligence

How does the merchant onboarding work?

30 minutes to start.

1) Merchant downloads app or visits web dashboard
2) Scans QR code provided by REZ
3) Links UPI account (for payouts)
4) REZ generates their branded QR
5) Customer scans → pays → earns 5% coins

No POS hardware needed. Works with any phone.

Business Model

How does REZ make money?

Three revenue streams:

1) Core Transaction Fee (5%): Merchant pays 5% on every REZ transaction. Customer gets 5% cashback. REZ keeps margin.

2) Promoted Sales (+2-5%): On AI-driven incremental sales. Merchants pay only when we deliver customers they wouldn't have found otherwise.

3) SaaS Subscriptions (₹299-₹2,999/mo): Advanced AI insights, white-label, API access for larger merchants.

Why will merchants pay 5%?

Because it's cheaper than alternatives:

- Google Ads: ₹500+ CAC, zero customer data
- Swiggy/Zomato: 20-30%, zero customer data
- REZ: 5%, full customer data, AI predictions

The math: A restaurant with ₹3L monthly revenue pays ₹15K to REZ. They get 200+ customer profiles, repeat rate tracking, and AI-powered offers. That's a bargain vs. burning ₹50K on ineffective ads.

What are your unit economics?

From our beta:

- AOV: ₹650
- ARPU: ₹195/month
- LTV: ₹3,900
- CAC: ₹76
- LTV:CAC: 51x

This is the foundation. Scale improves everything.

When do you become profitable?

Per cluster: 6-8 months.

A "cluster" is ~200 merchants in one neighborhood. We activate 50-100 merchants initially, then grow organically. Once density hits ~200 merchants, the network effect kicks in: customer retention rises, CAC drops, revenue per merchant increases.

Company-level: We project profitability by Year 4 at current growth model.

Market & Competition

How big is the market?

TAM: ₹42 Lakh Crore
India's local commerce market (SMB retail, services, food)

SAM: ₹8.4 Lakh Crore
Addressable: digitally-aware SMBs in urban India

Year 3 target: ₹200 Cr ARR
120 cities × 40,000 merchants

Who are your competitors?

Direct competitors: None at our scale and vision. Most "loyalty" startups are single-merchant or single-category.

Indirect:
- Aggregators (Swiggy, Zomato): Own transactions, not relationships. We complement, not compete.
- Google/My Business: Listings, not transactions. No loyalty layer.
- CRED: Consumer-focused credit card rewards. We're merchant-focused.

Real moat: No one is building the behavioral graph of local commerce at our scale.

What if Swiggy/Zomato copies this?

Three reasons they can't easily replicate:

1) Incentive problem: Aggregators make money by taking a cut. REZ makes money when merchants grow. We're aligned with merchants; they're extractive.

2) Data ownership: Merchants don't trust aggregators with their data. They'd never give Swiggy their customer list—it's their only competitive advantage.

3) Network effects: Our behavioral graph gets smarter with every transaction. Aggregators would need to rebuild from scratch with a different incentive model.

Risks & Challenges

What could go wrong?

Biggest risks:

1) Cold start: Merchants won't join without consumers; consumers won't join without merchants. Mitigation: Start in small neighborhoods. Activate 50-100 merchants per cluster before marketing to consumers.

2) Merchant churn: If repeat rates stay low, merchants lose faith. Mitigation: Focus on repeat rate as north star. Don't chase GMV.

3) Copycats with more capital: Big tech enters. Mitigation: Build data flywheel fast. By the time they understand what we're doing, we'll have 2 years of behavioral data.

What's your regulatory risk?

Low.

- We're not a payment processor (UPI handles payments)
- We're not a financial institution
- REZ Coins are rewards, not currency
- No KYC required for basic transactions

If we expand to BNPL or credit: We'll need appropriate licenses. That's a future conversation.

How do you handle fraud?

Multi-layer approach:

1) Device fingerprinting: Prevent multiple accounts per device
2) Behavioral analysis: ML models flag suspicious patterns (instant redemptions, geo anomalies)
3) Merchant verification: GST, bank account verification before onboarding
4) Real-time limits: New users have lower limits until trust is established

Our skin in the game: REZ earns more when merchants grow. Fraud hurts everyone.
